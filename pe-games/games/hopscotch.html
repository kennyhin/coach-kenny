Hopscotch

Grades K-3

📖 How to Play

  • Draw a hopscotch grid on the ground with chalk (or use tape indoors).
  • Numbers 1-8 (or 1-10) in a traditional pattern: single, double, single, double.
  • Player tosses a marker (beanbag, stone) into square 1.
  • Hop on one foot over square 1, landing on single squares with one foot and double squares with two feet.
  • At the end, turn around and hop back, picking up the marker on the way.
  • If you step on a line, lose your foot, or miss the marker — next player's turn!

🎯 Variations

  • Number Call: Teacher calls a number — hop to that number and back.
  • Spelling Hop: Letters instead of numbers — spell words by hopping.
  • Math Hop: Squares have math problems — solve before hopping.
  • Partner Hop: Two players, alternating turns, racing to complete the course.

🧰 Equipment

  • Sidewalk chalk (outdoor) or painter's tape (indoor)
  • Beanbag or small stone as a marker
  • Multiple grids for more students to play simultaneously
Nevada PE Standards

NVPE S1.E3: Jump and land in both horizontal and vertical planes with control.

NVPE S2.E1: Demonstrates spatial awareness and balance during movement.

NVPE S4.E4: Applies rules and fair play during game activities.
